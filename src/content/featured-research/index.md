In high-stakes clinical settings like the Intensive Care Unit (ICU), AI models must know when to say "I don't know".

My recent work introduces a novel multi-modal framework, grounded in Belief Function Theory, to reliably predict patient outcomes. By fusing structured Electronic Health Records (EHRs) with unstructured clinical notes, this architecture explicitly models evidential uncertainty.

This approach not only improves diagnostic accuracy but flags unreliable AI predictions, allowing the system to safely defer to human doctors. It is a critical step toward deploying transparent, human-in-the-loop AI in hospitals.
